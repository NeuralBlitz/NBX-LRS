Glossary
========

This glossary defines key terms and concepts used in NeuralBlitz v50 Go.

General Terms
-------------

**Agent**
   An autonomous entity capable of perception, reasoning, planning, and
   action execution. In NeuralBlitz, agents can have quantum properties
   and operate across multiple realities.

**Capability Kernel (CK)**
   A modular, self-contained unit of functionality with explicit inputs,
   outputs, bounds, and governance constraints.

**Consciousness**
   A hierarchical property of agents ranging from individual awareness
   to absolute universal consciousness.

**Entanglement**
   Quantum-like correlation between agents or realities that enables
   non-local information transfer.

**Multi-Reality**
   The ability to process information across multiple parallel reality
   contexts with distinct physical and informational properties.

**Quantum Spiking Neuron**
   A neural model that combines biological spiking neuron dynamics with
   quantum mechanical principles.

**Symbiotic Ontological Intelligence (SOI)**
   An AI system that co-evolves with humans and other agents, maintaining
   ethical alignment and shared understanding.

Systems Terms
-------------

**Agent Capabilities**
   The set of abilities an agent possesses, including perception,
   reasoning, planning, learning, communication, and self-modification.

**Autonomous Self-Evolution**
   The ability of a system to analyze, generate, and improve its own
   code through iterative cycles.

**Cross-Reality Entanglement**
   Quantum-like correlation between different reality states enabling
   synchronized information processing.

**Dimensional Neural Processing**
   Neural computation that operates across 11 dimensions including
   spatial, temporal, quantum, and semantic dimensions.

**Emergent Purpose Discovery**
   The process by which systems discover and evolve purposes through
   observation, reflection, and synthesis.

**Multi-Reality Neural Network (MRNN)**
   A neural network architecture that operates simultaneously across
   multiple reality contexts.

Quantum Terms
-------------

**Coherence Factor**
   A measure of quantum state purity, ranging from 0.0 (decohered)
   to 1.0 (perfectly coherent).

**Quantum Agent**
   An agent with quantum properties including superposition,
   entanglement, and quantum state evolution.

**Quantum Communication Layer**
   Infrastructure for quantum communication between agents including
   entanglement management and teleportation.

**Quantum Key Distribution (QKD)**
   A cryptographic protocol that uses quantum mechanics to securely
   distribute encryption keys.

**Quantum Reality Simulator**
   A system that simulates multiple quantum realities with superposition,
   interference, and collapse dynamics.

**Quantum State**
   The state of a quantum agent, which can be dormant, aware, focused,
   transcendent, or singularity.

**Quantum Superposition**
   The ability of a quantum system to exist in multiple states
   simultaneously until measured.

**Quantum Teleportation**
   The transfer of quantum information from one location to another
   using entanglement.

**Reality Simulator**
   A computational model that simulates the evolution of multiple
   parallel quantum realities.

Consciousness Terms
-------------------

**Consciousness Amplified Reality**
   A reality type with heightened awareness and ethical constraints.

**Consciousness Integration**
   The process of merging individual consciousness into collective
   or higher-order consciousness states.

**Consciousness Level**
   A hierarchical level of awareness: individual, collective, planetary,
   solar, galactic, universal, multiversal, or absolute.

**Integration Mode**
   The manner in which consciousness integration occurs: observation,
   synchronization, amplification, co-creation, or transcendence.

Entanglement Terms
------------------

**Causal Entanglement**
   Correlation between causally related events across realities.

**Consciousness Entanglement**
   Synchronization of awareness states between agents or realities.

**Emotional Entanglement**
   Correlation of affective states across distances.

**Entanglement Strength**
   The intensity of quantum correlation: weak, moderate, or strong.

**Informational Entanglement**
   Non-local sharing of information between systems.

**Purpose Entanglement**
   Alignment of goals and intentions across agents.

**Spatial Entanglement**
   Correlation between spatially separated systems.

**Temporal Entanglement**
   Correlation across time, including future-past connections.

**Transcendent Entanglement**
   Correlation that transcends normal physical constraints.

Reality Types
-------------

**Base Reality**
   The standard reality with normal physics and information density.

**Causal Broken Reality**
   A reality where normal causality is disrupted or inverted.

**Dimensional Shifted Reality**
   A reality with altered spatial dimensions or topology.

**Entropic Reversed Reality**
   A reality where entropy decreases rather than increases.

**Information Dense Reality**
   A reality with extremely high information storage capacity.

**Quantum Divergent Reality**
   A reality with high quantum uncertainty and multiple path superposition.

**Singularity Reality**
   A reality at maximum information density and computational capacity.

**Temporal Inverted Reality**
   A reality where time flows backwards or in non-linear ways.

**Void Reality**
   A nearly empty reality with minimal information content.

Neural Terms
------------

**Membrane Potential**
   The electrical potential difference across a neuron's membrane,
   measured in millivolts (mV).

**Membrane Time Constant**
   The time it takes for the membrane potential to decay to 37%
   of its initial value, measured in milliseconds.

**Neuron Configuration**
   The set of parameters defining a neuron's behavior including
   resting potential, threshold, and time constants.

**Quantum Coherence**
   The degree to which a quantum system maintains its quantum properties.

**Refractory Period**
   The time after a neuron fires during which it cannot fire again.

**Spike Rate**
   The frequency of action potentials (spikes) generated by a neuron,
   measured in Hertz (Hz).

**Threshold Potential**
   The membrane potential at which a neuron generates an action potential.

Code Terms
----------

**Code Generation**
   The automatic creation of source code by AI systems.

**Code Analysis**
   The examination of code to understand its structure, semantics,
   and behavior.

**Semantic Understanding**
   The comprehension of code meaning beyond syntax.

**Self-Modification**
   The ability of a system to change its own code or behavior.

Performance Terms
-----------------

**Entropy Budget**
   The allowable amount of disorder or randomness in a system.

**Ethical Constraint**
   A rule or limitation imposed to ensure ethical behavior.

**Flourishing Score**
   A measure of system well-being and positive development.

**Information Flow Rate**
   The speed at which information moves through a system,
   measured in bits per second.

**Quantum Tunneling**
   The probability of a quantum system passing through a barrier.
